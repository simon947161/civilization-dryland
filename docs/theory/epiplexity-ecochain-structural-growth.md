# 2. Beyond Classification

Traditional modern science is largely based on classification.

Human civilization separated knowledge into:

- physics
- biology
- economics
- ecology
- psychology
- engineering

This approach allowed humanity to reduce complexity and develop controllable systems.

However, real-world systems rarely behave independently.

In environmental practice, wind affects moisture.
Moisture affects vegetation.
Vegetation affects temperature.
Temperature affects energy demand.
Energy systems affect social behavior.
Social behavior affects governance.
Governance influences land structure.

These systems continuously overlap and influence one another.

The world is not merely composed of isolated objects.

It behaves more like:

> a continuously interacting structural field.
